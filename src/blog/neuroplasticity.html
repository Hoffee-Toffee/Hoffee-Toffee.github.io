Neuroplasticity

Growth Mindset

What is it?

Neuroplasticity is the ability of the brain to change due to experience.
It is very relevant to learning, as to learn you need to adapt to and absorb new knowledge and experience.

Neuroplasticity is commonly paired with the idea of a 'growth mindset', which is all about how to handle setbacks and approach problems to improve your skills.
Improving the plasticity of your brain has mainly benefits, including streamlining learning, improved memory and concentration, keener cognitive skills, and can aid struggles with some mental health issues.

In Practice

There are two main classes of activities you can do to increase your plasticity.
Cognitive and physical exercise, and cognitive reflection.

This includes activities such as learning new skills, resting frequently, and going for runs or walks in new places/routes.
For me, I'll likely go for walks, create music (albeit shoddy), and exploring new programming concepts.
